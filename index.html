A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

Biography

Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam (15 October 1931 â€“ 27 July 2015), better known as A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, was an Indian scientist who served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. He was a renowned aerospace engineer and played a pivotal role in India's civilian space program and military missile development.

Dr. Kalam was born and raised in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu. He graduated from Madras Institute of Technology and later joined the Defense Research and Development Organization (DRDO) and Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO). Kalam was instrumental in the development of India's first satellite launch vehicle (SLV) and several other important space and missile technologies.

Apart from his scientific achievements, Kalam was also known for his vision to transform India into a developed nation. He often interacted with students and encouraged them to pursue science and technology. He wrote several inspirational books and received numerous awards and honors, including the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian award.
